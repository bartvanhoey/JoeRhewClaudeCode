## Founding Story

- **Founded:** Incorporated **January 26, 2021** in Delaware, per Wikipedia's sourcing of corporate filings. ([Wikipedia](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Anthropic)) Some secondary press summaries instead say "February 2021" ([GreyJournal](https://greyjournal.net/hustle/inspire/anthropic-ipo-dario-amodei-founder-lessons/)) — likely the difference between legal incorporation date and when the founding was publicly known/announced. Treat "early 2021" as the safe high-level claim; use Jan 26, 2021 as the more precise, filings-backed date.
- **Location:** San Francisco, California (still HQ today).
- **Founders:** Anthropic was founded by a group of former OpenAI employees, led by siblings:
  - **Dario Amodei** — former VP of Research at OpenAI; became Anthropic's CEO.
  - **Daniela Amodei** — former VP of Safety & Policy at OpenAI; became Anthropic's President.
  - Other co-founders consistently named across sources: **Jared Kaplan, Jack Clark, Sam McCandlish, Benjamin (Tom) Mann, Tom Brown, Christopher Olah**. ([Wikipedia](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Anthropic), [AI Mode](https://aimode.co/anthropic-founders/))
  - Note: source counts of "founders" range from 5 to 8 (some count just the Amodeis + 4, others list up to 7-8 names, and one secondary source says "14 colleagues" left OpenAI around the same time without all being formal co-founders). The Amodei siblings plus Kaplan, Clark, McCandlish, and Mann are the names that appear most consistently as core co-founders.
- **Backstory / why they left OpenAI:** The founders departed OpenAI citing "directional differences" — commonly reported as disagreement over OpenAI's increasing commercialization and partnership with Microsoft, which the founders felt diluted its safety-first mission. ([Wikipedia](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Anthropic), [Contrary Research](https://research.contrary.com/company/anthropic))
- **Initial funding:** Raised **$124 million** in its first announced funding round (May 2021), from backers including Jaan Tallinn (Skype co-founder), Dustin Moskovitz (Facebook co-founder, via Open Philanthropy), and Eric Schmidt. Confirmed directly on Anthropic's blog. ([Anthropic](https://www.anthropic.com/news/anthropic-raises-124-million-to-build-more-reliable-general-ai-systems))

## Legal Structure: Public Benefit Corporation (PBC)

- Anthropic is incorporated in Delaware as a **Public Benefit Corporation** — a for-profit legal structure (not a nonprofit) that, under Delaware law, explicitly permits company directors to balance **stockholders' financial interests** against a stated **public benefit purpose** and the interests of those materially affected by the company's conduct. ([Britannica](https://www.britannica.com/money/Anthropic-PBC), [Anthropic](https://www.anthropic.com/news/the-long-term-benefit-trust))
- Anthropic's certificate of incorporation states its public benefit purpose as: **"the responsible development and maintenance of advanced AI for the long-term benefit of humanity."** ([Anthropic — Long-Term Benefit Trust announcement](https://www.anthropic.com/news/the-long-term-benefit-trust))
- **Why they chose PBC status:** Per Anthropic's own explanation, the PBC form gives the board "legal latitude to weigh long- and short-term externalities of decisions...alongside the financial interests of our stockholders" — i.e., it reduces the risk that the board could be sued by shareholders for prioritizing safety/societal impact over short-term profit maximization. ([Anthropic](https://www.anthropic.com/news/the-long-term-benefit-trust), summarized also by [Time](https://time.com/6983420/anthropic-structure-openai-incentives/))
- **The Long-Term Benefit Trust (LTBT):** Anthropic supplemented the PBC structure with an additional governance layer — the Long-Term Benefit Trust — because PBC status alone only makes balancing public interest *legally permissible* for directors, not *required* or independently enforced.
  - The LTBT is composed of **trustees with no financial stake in Anthropic**, selected for expertise in AI safety, national security, public policy, and social enterprise (trustees named in sources: Neil Buddy Shah, Richard Fontaine, Mariano-Florentino Cuéllar; the company page also frames it as up to five trustees). ([Anthropic](https://www.anthropic.com/news/the-long-term-benefit-trust))
  - It operates via a special class of stock (**Class T Common Stock**) that gives trustees the right to elect and remove a growing share of Anthropic's board — reaching the ability to elect a **majority of the board within four years** of the relevant trigger.
  - Purpose, in Anthropic's words: to ensure leadership weighs catastrophic-risk and safety considerations rather than "prioritizing being the first to market above all other objectives."
  - Includes "failsafe" provisions letting stockholders amend/override the structure via supermajority vote.
- Net effect for GTM messaging: Anthropic can point to a concrete governance mechanism (not just a mission statement) as evidence its safety commitments are structurally backed, which differentiates it from purely investor-controlled competitors.

## Headquarters and Office Locations

- **Global HQ:** 500 Howard Street, San Francisco, California. ([Wikipedia](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Anthropic))
- Anthropic's own careers page notes "most staff are in the Bay Area and come to the office regularly," with some employees commuting from further away for one week a month — indicating SF remains the cultural/operational center even as the company globalizes. ([Anthropic Careers](https://www.anthropic.com/careers))
- Reporting in 2025–2026 describes rapid office expansion. As of mid-2026, secondary sources describe Anthropic offices/presence across roughly a dozen cities, including:
  - **US:** San Francisco (HQ), Seattle, New York City, Washington D.C., Boston
  - **Europe:** London, Dublin, Zurich, Paris, Munich, Brussels
  - **Asia-Pacific:** Tokyo, Seoul, Singapore, Bengaluru/Bangalore, Sydney
  - **Other:** offices noted in British Columbia and Ontario (Canada)
  ([Clay dossier](https://www.clay.com/dossier/anthropic-headquarters-office-locations), [SiliconANGLE](https://siliconangle.com/2025/09/26/anthropic-triple-international-headcount-add-offices-following-latest-funding-round/), [Anthropic — Paris/Munich announcement](https://www.anthropic.com/news/new-offices-in-paris-and-munich-expand-european-presence))
- Anthropic announced in 2025 plans to **triple its international headcount** and open new offices in Europe and Asia following a funding round, and is reportedly expanding into a larger San Francisco footprint (reports of taking an entire downtown SF office building by early 2026). ([allwork.space](https://allwork.space/2025/09/anthropic-to-triple-global-workforce-open-new-offices-in-europe-and-asia/))
- **Caveat:** the exact, current list of office cities is not something Anthropic publishes as a single authoritative page (other than its jobs board filter); the list above is aggregated from multiple secondary sources and may not be fully current or complete.

## Company Size / Headcount

Anthropic does not publish an official, regularly updated headcount as a private company, so estimates vary by source and date:

- **2023:** ~521 employees (cited retrospectively). ([Revelio Labs via search aggregation](https://www.reveliolabs.com/companies/anthropic-pbc/employees/))
- **December 2025:** Estimates diverge significantly by source:
  - ~2,300 employees (Fortune, Dec 2025)
  - ~3,476 employees (Revelio Labs, Dec 2025) — implies ~90% YoY growth from 2024
  - ~2,500 employees (per one Wikipedia-sourced 2026 figure)
- **March 2026:** ~5,000 employees per Tracxn data cited by SaaStr — title of that piece specifically frames Anthropic as remarkably lean/efficient relative to its valuation and revenue. ([SaaStr](https://www.saastr.com/anthropic-only-has-5000-employees-almost-no-one-has-ever-been-this-efficient-thats-by-choice/))
- **Why the spread:** Different sources count differently — full-time employees only vs. including contractors/extended workforce, and data-vendor estimates (Revelio, Tracxn) vs. press-reported figures can lag or lead actual counts given Anthropic's reported hypergrowth.
- **Takeaway for GTM use:** Cite a range (roughly **2,300–5,000 employees as of late 2025/early 2026**) rather than a single number, and always date-stamp any headcount claim since this is one of the fastest-moving facts about the company.

## Org Structure / Leadership (as publicly known)

Per Anthropic's own "Company" page, the organization frames its work around three broad areas: ([Anthropic](https://www.anthropic.com/company))
- **Research** — frontier AI research and safety techniques
- **Policy** — engagement with policymakers on AI's societal impacts
- **Product** — building and shipping Claude and related tools

### Key leadership (as of research date, June 2026)
- **Dario Amodei** — Co-founder & CEO
- **Daniela Amodei** — Co-founder & President
- **Jared Kaplan** — Co-founder; Chief Science Officer
- **Jack Clark** — Co-founder; Head of Policy (former OpenAI policy director and tech journalist) ([Wikipedia — Jack Clark](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Jack_Clark_(AI_policy_expert)))
- **Mike Krieger** — Instagram co-founder/CTO; joined Anthropic as Chief Product Officer in **May 2024** ([Anthropic announcement](https://anthropic.com/news/mike-krieger-joins-anthropic), [TechCrunch](https://techcrunch.com/2024/05/15/anthropic-hires-instagram-co-founder-as-head-of-product)). Per several 2026 secondary sources, Krieger has since moved to co-lead a newly created **"Anthropic Labs"** unit (an experimental products incubator reportedly formed January 2026), with **Ami Vora** stepping up as Chief Product Officer. **This leadership transition is reported only in secondary/aggregator sources I could not directly verify on anthropic.com — flag as unconfirmed/recent and worth re-checking before using in client-facing material.**
- **Tom Brown** — co-founder; reported in some 2026 sources as "Chief Compute Officer" — also not independently confirmed on Anthropic's own site in this research pass.
- **Board of Directors** (per Anthropic's official Company page): Dario Amodei, Daniela Amodei, Yasmin Razavi, Reed Hastings, Chris Liddell, Vas Narasimhan. ([Anthropic](https://www.anthropic.com/company))
- **Long-Term Benefit Trust trustees:** Neil Buddy Shah, Richard Fontaine, Mariano-Florentino Cuéllar (and reportedly up to 5 total seats). ([Anthropic](https://www.anthropic.com/company))

**Caveat:** Leadership/org details change quickly at a company growing this fast. The core founder-executives (Dario, Daniela, Kaplan, Clark) are well-confirmed via Anthropic's own site; more granular C-suite titles and the "Anthropic Labs" reorg are sourced from press aggregators of varying reliability and should be re-verified close to time of use.

3. Current headcount: no single authoritative number; estimates for late 2025/early 2026 range ~2,300–5,000 depending on source and what's counted.
