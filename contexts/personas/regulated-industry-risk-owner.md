# Persona: The Regulated-Industry Risk/Compliance Owner

**Who they are:** Compliance officers, actuaries, General Counsel/legal ops, and clinical/regulatory affairs staff working inside finance, healthcare, life sciences, legal, and government. Distinct from the IT/security buyer — this persona owns *regulatory and professional* risk, not infrastructure/data risk.

## Pain points & use cases
- Needs AI output that is defensible, auditable, and explainable in a regulatory or legal context, not just "helpful."
- Named compliance-adjacent use cases: KYC/AML, reserve adequacy and underwriting/covenant analysis, regulatory gap analysis, clinical/regulatory documentation, FedRAMP-High data handling.
- Fears AI replacing professional judgment or introducing malpractice-level errors — wants augmentation, not automation of the final call.

## Messaging hooks Anthropic uses
- Legal: *"Practice better judgment with Claude."* / *"Claude handles research, drafting, and the assembly of legal work, so your team can focus on the calls only they can make."* (https://claude.com/solutions/legal)
- Financial services: *"Your financial competitive edge, from signal to decision."* (https://claude.com/solutions/financial-services)
- Government: FedRAMP-High authorization; data-control-first, procurement/compliance-led messaging (https://www.anthropic.com/news/expanding-access-to-claude-for-government)
- Recurring rhetorical move across this group: explicitly reassuring that AI augments professional judgment rather than replacing it — most pronounced in Legal ("the calls only they can make").
- Case-study framing consistently quantifies *time saved*, not headcount reduction (e.g., Novo Nordisk: clinical documentation time cut ~90%; IG Group: 70 analyst-hours/week saved) — deliberately avoids displacement language.
