# Persona: The Knowledge Worker / Non-Technical Operator

**Who they are:** Analysts, operations staff, marketers, finance, and legal-adjacent employees who work with documents and data but don't code. Anthropic's own 81,000-user qualitative study calls out an overlapping "independent workers" cluster (freelancers, entrepreneurs, small business owners) who report the strongest perceived economic benefit from Claude.

## Pain points & use cases
- Document-heavy work: research, drafting, review, redlining, reconciliation — wants the benefit of AI agents without needing to write code or use developer tooling.
- Felt underserved by Claude Code, which is explicitly developer-oriented — there was a recognized persona gap between "developers who'll use Claude Code" and "knowledge workers who need something simpler."
- Values "cognitive partnership" (brainstorming, problem-solving) almost as much as raw productivity — Anthropic's research found this the #2 most-cited benefit (17%), just behind productivity (32%).

## Messaging hooks Anthropic uses
- Claude Cowork: *"a simpler way for anyone — not just developers — to work with Claude."* (https://claude.com/product/cowork)
- Explicitly framed as targeting "researchers, analysts, operations teams, legal professionals, finance teams."
- Tone: de-technicalized, accessibility-focused — simplicity and approachability over speed/power framing.
