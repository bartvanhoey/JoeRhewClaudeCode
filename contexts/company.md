# Anthropic — Company

## Founding & Structure

- **Founded:** January 2021, Delaware (incorporated Jan 26, 2021; some press says "Feb 2021" — treat "early 2021" as the safe claim).
- **Founders:** Siblings **Dario Amodei** (ex-OpenAI VP of Research → CEO) and **Daniela Amodei** (ex-OpenAI VP of Safety & Policy → President), plus **Jared Kaplan, Jack Clark, Sam McCandlish, Benjamin "Tom" Mann, Tom Brown, Christopher Olah**. Left OpenAI over "directional differences" — widely reported as disagreement with OpenAI's commercialization/Microsoft partnership diluting its safety-first mission.
- **First funding:** $124M seed round (May 2021) from Jaan Tallinn, Dustin Moskovitz/Open Philanthropy, and Eric Schmidt, among others.
- **Legal structure:** Delaware **Public Benefit Corporation (PBC)** — a for-profit structure that legally permits directors to balance stockholder financial interests against a stated public benefit purpose. Anthropic's certificate of incorporation states that purpose as: *"the responsible development and maintenance of advanced AI for the long-term benefit of humanity."* PBC status makes weighing public interest legally permissible, not mandatory — hence the Trust below.
- **Long-Term Benefit Trust (LTBT):** An added governance layer of financially-disinterested trustees (named: Neil Buddy Shah, Richard Fontaine, Mariano-Florentino Cuéllar; up to five seats) selected for expertise in AI safety, national security, and public policy. Via a special **Class T** stock class, the Trust can elect/remove a growing share of Anthropic's board, reaching majority control within four years of the trigger. Purpose: keep leadership weighing catastrophic-risk and safety considerations over pure first-to-market pressure. Stockholders retain a supermajority-vote override/failsafe.
- **GTM angle:** This gives Anthropic a concrete governance mechanism — not just a mission statement — to back up its safety claims, differentiating it from investor-controlled competitors.

## Headquarters & Footprint

- **Global HQ:** 500 Howard Street, San Francisco, CA. Most staff are Bay Area-based and come into the office regularly; SF remains the cultural/operational center.
- **Other offices (2025–2026 expansion):**
  - US: Seattle, New York City, Washington D.C., Boston
  - Europe: London, Dublin, Zurich, Paris, Munich, Brussels
  - Asia-Pacific: Tokyo, Seoul, Singapore, Bengaluru, Sydney
  - Also reported: British Columbia and Ontario, Canada
- In 2025, Anthropic announced plans to triple international headcount and is reportedly expanding its SF footprint (an entire downtown office building by early 2026).
- *Caveat: no single authoritative office list is published by Anthropic; the above is aggregated from secondary sources and may not be fully current.*

## Size & Scale

Anthropic is private and doesn't publish official headcount; estimates vary widely by source/date:

- 2023: ~520 employees
- Dec 2025: estimates range ~2,300–3,500 depending on source
- March 2026: ~5,000 employees (Tracxn/SaaStr), framed as notably lean relative to valuation and revenue

**For GTM use:** cite a range (~2,300–5,000 employees, late 2025/early 2026) and date-stamp the claim — headcount is one of the fastest-moving facts about this company.
